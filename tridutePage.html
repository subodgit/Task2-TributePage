Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam Azad

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam Azad, also known as the "Missile Man of India," was an esteemed scientist, author, and the 11th President of India. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.

Dr. Kalam played a crucial role in India's defense and space research programs. He was a key figure in the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle and nuclear weapons. His contributions to the field of science and technology earned him several prestigious awards and honors, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award.
